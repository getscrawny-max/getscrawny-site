Scrawny's Library

Free lesson resources for nonspeaking, minimally speaking, and unreliably speaking spellers building access to communication through purposeful motor practice.

These lessons are designed for learners whose speech may not reliably show what they know. Each template supports spelling or typing as communication, presumes competence, and gives practitioners and families a structured way to pair rich content with regulation, motor planning, and meaningful response.

Lesson web

Lessons for communication, fluency, and autonomy.

Use the library as a starting point for letterboard, stencil, keyboard, or typed-response practice. The goal is not performance for its own sake; it is access to thought, education, relationship, and self-directed participation.
